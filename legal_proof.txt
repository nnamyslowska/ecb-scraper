Legal proof: Web Scraping of the European Central Bank (ECB) Website
Website: https://www.ecb.europa.eu
Date: April 2026

1. EU OPEN DATA DIRECTIVE (2019/1024/EU)
The EU Open Data Directive (Directive 2019/1024/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council of 20 June 2019 on open data and the re-use of public sector information) establishes that documents held by public sector bodies should be made available for re-use. The ECB, as an EU institution, falls under this framework. The directive explicitly encourages machine-readable access to public sector information.

2. ECB ROBOTS.TXT ANALYSIS
The ECB robots.txt file (https://www.ecb.europa.eu/robots.txt) was checked on April 2026. Key findings:
- User-agent: * (applies to all bots)
- Sitemap: https://www.ecb.europa.eu/sitemap.xml (ECB provides a sitemap)
- Crawl-delay: 5 (ECB requests 5 seconds between requests — I respect this in the project)
- Disallowed paths: only specific content-fragment URLs (*_content.xx.html) and internal media files are disallowed
- NO general prohibition on scraping
- NO prohibition on AI/ML data collection
- The ECB explicitly provides a sitemap, indicating they expect automated access

My scraper respects all robots.txt rules:
- Obey the 5-second crawl delay
- Does not access any disallowed paths
- Identify ourselves with a standard user agent

3. ECB RSS FEEDS
The ECB explicitly provides RSS feeds for machine usage: https://www.ecb.europa.eu/home/html/rss.en.html
RSS (Really Simple Syndication) feeds are specifically designed to be read by automated tools and programs. By providing these feeds, the ECB is explicitly inviting machine-based access to their content.

4. ECB COPYRIGHT POLICY
ECB publications are subject to the ECB copyright policy, which permits:
- Reproduction for educational and non-commercial research purposes
- Proper attribution must be given to the ECB as the source

My project is:
- Non-commercial (academic research for a university course)
- Educational
- Properly attributed (all data clearly sourced from ECB)

5. NATURE OF DATA COLLECTED
- All scraped content consists of publicly available press releases and speeches
- No personal data is collected
- No login or authentication is required to access the content
- No technical protection measures are bypassed

6. SCRAPING METHODOLOGY
The scraper follows best practices for polite scraping:
- Respects the Crawl-delay of 5 seconds specified in robots.txt
- Uses a standard user agent string
- Makes a limited number of requests
- Does not attempt to bypass any access restrictions
- Only accesses publicly available pages

Based on the above analysis, scraping the ECB website for this academic
project is legal under EU law and compliant with the ECB's published
access policies.
